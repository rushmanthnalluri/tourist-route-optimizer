1 Introduction
Planning an optimal tourist itinerary involves multiple complex variables: distance, time, budget, weather, and dynamic crowds. The Tourist Route Planner employs a Hybrid AI approach to solve this multi-objective optimization problem. By layering Uninformed/Informed Search, Constraint Satisfaction, Adversarial Reasoning, and Probabilistic inference, the system generates real-time, explainable, and optimal tourist routes.

4. Algorithms and Their Uses

1. Uninformed & Informed Search

Algorithms: BFS, DFS, Uniform Cost Search, A* Search

Uses: Used as the foundational Path Finding and Route Optimization engine. The A* algorithm utilizes Haversine distance heuristics to find the shortest geographical path between tourist attractions while expanding minimal nodes.

2. Constraint Satisfaction (CSP)

Algorithms: Backtracking, Min-Conflicts

Uses: Applied for Itinerary Scheduling. It ensures tourists visit locations during valid operational hours (Morning, Afternoon, Evening) without overlaps, respecting hard constraints (budget/time).

3. Adversarial Search

Algorithms: Minimax Algorithm, Alpha-Beta Pruning

Uses: Strategic Decision Making. Models the environment as an adversary (e.g., traffic jams, sudden closures) to compute robust fallback paths and Expected Utility of alternative routes.

4. Probabilistic Models

Algorithms: Bayesian Networks, Hidden Markov Models (HMM)

Uses: Uncertainty Handling. Bayesian inference predicts crowd levels based on weather probabilities. HMM tracks the latent state of the tourist (moving vs. visiting) based on noisy GPS emissions.

5. Explainability & Ethics

Techniques: Trace Viewer, Rule Engine, Ethical Heuristics

Uses: Transparency. Provides step-by-step visual traces of AI decision trees (Expanded Nodes, F-costs). Enforces ethical routing to avoid hazardous zones or restricted areas.
